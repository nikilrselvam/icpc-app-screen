#### What’s your favorite problem? It does not have to be an algorithm, or even CS related!
>This is one of the most interesting problems I encountered when I was in high school:
>
>Q: You are on a small boat, isolated in the middle of the ocean. The only way you can save yourself is by throwing two flares in the sky exactly 45 minutes apart. You only have two flares (which cannot be used for anything other than throwing), so you want to be smart about how you use them. To help you measure time, you have a lighter and two irregularly shaped ropes which you may burn. You know that each rope takes exactly 60 minutes to completely burn. Assuming that the time taken to light the ropes/flares and throwing them does not count, how will you time the 45 minutes? (There is no way to know how much time has elapsed by looking at the ropes since they are completely irregular and assume that after spending so many days out in the ocean, your sense of time has degraded from bad to extremely poor).
>
>Solution (Spoiler):
>Light the first rope from both ends and the second rope from one end. At this time throw the first flare into the sky.
>When the first rope completely finishes burning, you have completed 30 minutes. At this point, immediately light the other end of the second rope. The second rope must have burned half its time (30 minutes) already and now burning from both ends will completely burn out in another 15 minutes. At this point in time, you have successfully timed 45 minutes, so toss the second flare into the sky!
