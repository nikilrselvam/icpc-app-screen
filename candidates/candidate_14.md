## ICPC Questions

#### Why ACM ICPC?
>In most of middle school and high school, I dedicated a lot of time to math competitions; over time, I came to realize that the best part about these competitions to me was the critical thinking and problem solving aspect, not necessarily the math itself. So as I started exploring CS, I was naturally drawn to competitive programming, seeing as it's pretty similar to math competitions in terms of it basically being about problem solving at its core. I've been excited about participating in ACM ICPC for a while because of this, and I've definitely had a great experience so far both with learning and just meeting others interested in problem solving contests like I am. Since competitive programming will likely stay an important activity to me throughout college, I'd like to be even more involved in the CP community here, which is why I am applying to intern with ICPC.

#### What’s your favorite problem? It does not have to be an algorithm, or even CS related!
>It's hard for me to pick one favorite problem, but one of them is the following math competition question (2008 USAMO #1):
>
>Prove that for each positive integer n, there exist pairwise relatively prime integers k0, k1, ..., kn such that k0*k1*...*kn - 1 is the product of two consecutive integers.
>
>I like this problem (and most of the others are for the same reason) because I enjoyed seeing how several relatively simple concepts came together nicely in my solution to prove an interesting result.
