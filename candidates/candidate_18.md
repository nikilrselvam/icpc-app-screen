#### What’s your favorite problem? It does not have to be an algorithm, or even CS related!
>I am enthralled by invariants, and any problems that involve them. The most obvious examples are the problems where numbers are erased and replaced on a blackboard. If you were to replace two numbers on a blackboard with the GCD and LCM, their product would be an invariant, and the sum would never decrease. Generally, invariants are such a nice way of looking at what could otherwise be very challenging problems, and result in elegant and efficient solutions. For example, the sum of the digits is invariant when taking mod 9, so any problems involving S(n) can be taken modulo 9 and made much easier. Invariants sum up what I love most about problem solving and computer science. They are examples of the number of different ways in which seemingly ugly problems can be tackled with beautiful and efficient solutions, often with just a little ingenuity or creativity.
>
