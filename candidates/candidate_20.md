## ICPC Questions

#### Why ACM ICPC?
>Frankly, I joined ACM without any direction or leaning. However, within a day of signing up for the membership, I found myself watching the Instagram takeover of ACM and was captivated by Nikil's interests and his contributions to ICPC. Soon, the advanced training workshop fully expanded my horizons on what the definition of "computing" is: it is an eye-widening one-second moment that comes from hours of practice. I was told to adopt a growth mindset in my LS7A (intro to cell biology) class, and that is to persistently seek discomfort and be eager to reach for more practice. It seems to me that joining the ICPC committee would train me in the art of staying calm in time-sensitive scenarios, to exercise logical interpreting in group settings, and essentially *throw* me a dictionary of new computing vocabulary. Fortunately, I am equally a good thrower and a good receiver--- I have taught middle-school students the basics of machine learning (what is machine learning? What is AI?) and robotics (using arduino). The teaching component of ICPC would fit perfectly with my experience, even though I am a freshman.

#### What’s your favorite problem? It does not have to be an algorithm, or even CS related!
>My favorite problem would have to come from the electron emission spectrum or atomic emission spectra. It is a direct visual representation of the electron's movement! It literally shows the electron jumping from one level to another and by knowing if it's a Lyman series or Balmer series you would know its starting level and thereby compute its ending level. I really enjoy figuring out a problem by knowing exact steps to approach the problem, and even more if I can see it (well, technically I haven't ever seen a single electron moving, but I can certainly draw a diagram of it)!
