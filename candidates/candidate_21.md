## ICPC Questions

#### Why ACM ICPC?
>I have always loved solving difficult problems, but breaking into competitive problem solving spheres was difficult at my high school as we had no math Olympiad or competitive programming. I see this as an opportunity to break into an organization which has always interested me.

#### What’s your favorite problem? It does not have to be an algorithm, or even CS related!
>My favorite problem is a follows: Is there a way to map every value in the real numbers to the natural numbers? I personally love this problem because of a personal experience. Understanding why this mapping doesn't exist and what that said about infinity had always baffled me until I was reading James Munkres's "Topology" that it all made sense to me.
>
>This is because the proof of the mapping's nonexistence relied on the properties of the two spaces. The fact that for any two real numbers there exists a number that is in between these two numbers makes the mapping impossible. You could just choose a new number that was in between the points f(n) and f(n+1).
>
>This taught me that the solution to problem solving is to never do more work than one has to. Find the exact minimum properties needed and only prove those. This valuable insight has greatly helped me both in math and life. But I hope to gain many more from ICPC

#### Do you have any past experience with teaching? (It is not required that you do!)
>I am the previous co-founder and president of Common Good Tutors, a tutoring group at my high school that offered free tutoring for students in need. As a result, I have hundreds of hours of tutoring experience in math, chemistry, biology, and computer science.
