## ICPC Questions

#### Why ACM ICPC?
>My primary reason for applying to ACM ICPC is that my favorite, and truthfully sometimes least favorite, part of computer science and coding is solving complicated problems. There is no better feeling than spending hours or maybe even days on a project and finally seeing the program run successfully for the very first time, and that feeling is pretty universal in regards to solving problems in general and the fact that there is an entire sub-organization that specializes in strengthening people's problem solving skills is exactly why ICPC stood out to me. While my approach to solving problems may be slower or faster than other people, I am very eager to both learn from others and develop my own problem solving skills, as well as to teach others how to solve problems that I am familiar with since I find the processes of learning and teaching to be equally rewarding.

#### What’s your favorite problem? It does not have to be an algorithm, or even CS related!
>I am a fan of problems where even though the problem may seem complicated, they actually have a pretty simple and straightforward solution. The first problem of this kind that came to mind would have to be the Tower of Hanoi problem. I first learned how to solve the problem in my first winter quarter at UCLA in PIC 10B, and I honestly really like how such a seemingly complicated problem can have such a simple (though somewhat tedious) solution.  I can also appreciate that the solution code also helped me study some of the major topics of the course right before my midterm.

#### Do you have any past experience with teaching? (It is not required that you do!)
>Yes I do! I mentioned in the previous page of questions, that I have some experience in teaching K-12 students when I worked as a Teacher's Assistant during my senior year, and another big part of why I am applying to ICPC is that I really enjoyed my time helping young, bright minds learn new things about various topics or to have the feeling of satisfaction that comes from aiding a student who was seriously struggling in a particular subject, a feeling that I can sympathize with.
