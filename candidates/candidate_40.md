## ICPC Questions

#### Why ACM ICPC?
>ACM ICPC in particular is exactly what I am looking for from a CS club because I love competitive programming and creative problem solving. I have been doing competitive programming since early high school and I have always found it to be something fun, yet also productive at the same time. When I first learned how to code, I was amazed at the nearly endless possibilities you could create and the total control you had as a programmer. Honestly, finding simple, elegant solutions to difficult problems through programming is truly beautiful. I already have some experience, I am a USACO Gold member, but I want to continue my competitive programming pursuits, and I know that ACM ICPC is the place to cultivate my passion. At the same time, I want to also give back to the club by teaching and advancing the club in the future, as an officer. Most importantly, I want to meet a lot of like-minded individuals like myself and form a strong community and network. Being an ACM ICPC Intern will be my first step in achieving these goals.

#### What’s your favorite problem? It does not have to be an algorithm, or even CS related!
>My favorite problem is having too much time on my hands because, while I feel unproductive, I am able to relax and really do whatever I want! Hopefully, being an ACM ICPC Intern will help me with that “problem”! However, my favorite algorithm is binary search. While relatively simple, binary search is a perfect example of that simple, elegant solution to a difficult problem that I mentioned earlier. It was one of the first algorithms that I was taught, and I found that it immediately, intuitively made sense to me and made the “complex” problem of quickly finding a specific number in a large array seem much simpler once the solution was made apparent. Now, binary search is not that complex to me, but learning about binary search was a foundational concept to me because it showed me that there is always a relatively simple solution to a complex problem; I just have to think creatively and analyze patterns to solve it.
