## ICPC Questions

#### Why ACM ICPC?
>When I first went to ACM ICPC's Beginner's Track, last winter, I didn't have any notion of how one could approach solving problems they had never seen before. Problem solving has always been an avid interest of mine, and the idea that it is a skill that you can learn and improve at was life-changing. On top of that, I’d found a passionate and welcoming community, dedicated not only to learning how to solve all of these new interesting problems, but to helping others do the same. Having seen firsthand the benefits ICPC can bring, I would find it an exciting and educative experience to be a part of sharing that with others.

#### What’s your favorite problem? It does not have to be an algorithm, or even CS related!
>One of my favourite problems is the quantum delayed choice experiment. It's really interesting because, as most unsolved problems in the world of quantum mechanics do, it could have really wacky implications about the nature of space and time1
>
>One worth mentioning that is competitive programming related, though, is a three part problem on Codeforces, I believe called the “Great Julya Calendar”. It’s really straight-forward to solve for input of size 10^6, but requires a few cool optimizations and tricks to get it to work for larger inputs, like 10^12 and 10^18. I’m sure these type of problems are common, but for me, it succinctly and for the first time illustrated both how dramatically problem difficulty can be affected by the size of the input, and in attempting to solve it and looking at the solution, how the same information can be acquired and conveyed in a variety of inventive ways. I really enjoyed and learned a lot from it!
